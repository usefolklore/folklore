## Prompt 1 — Hero Identifier

**Business (one sentence):**
> wellinformed is a local-first knowledge-graph + P2P federation
> layer that gives AI assistants persistent memory across
> sessions, sources, and machines — no GPU, no cloud, no SaaS
> dependency.

**Ideal customer:**
> Senior engineers, ML practitioners, and solo developers paying
> for Claude Code / Cursor / ChatGPT — who are tired of every
> session starting from zero, of paying API tokens to re-research
> the same papers, of sending proprietary code to OpenAI just to
> ask "what did I do last week?", and of watching their AI
> assistant forget the exact context they already paid to teach
> it.

**Dominant survival-level desire** (not a list of wants):
> Control. Specifically: a memory layer they own, not rent. In an
> era where every productivity tool is being annexed by SaaS, they
> want the substrate of their own work — what they've read, what
> they've built, what their AI has helped them figure out — to
> stay theirs.

**Website opening line (no cleverness, no brand name):**
> **Memory that belongs to you, not to OpenAI.**

## Prompt 2 — Three-Layer Problem Mapper

**External problem (the customer can name this):**
> "My AI assistant forgets everything between sessions. I keep
> re-explaining the same project, re-pasting the same code,
> re-citing the same papers."

**Internal problem (the frustration underneath):**
> "I'm doing the AI's job for it. I'm the human glue between
> conversations. The tool that's supposed to make me faster is
> making me into its memory layer — and I'm paying ChatGPT/Claude
> to NOT remember things I already taught it."

**Philosophical problem (the injustice they feel):**
> "Memory should be a primitive every user owns. Not a feature
> gated by a $200/mo enterprise tier that disappears when the
> provider rotates their pricing. Knowledge I've curated through
> my own work shouldn't live on someone else's server, where they
> can change the terms at any time."

**Three test subheadlines (one per layer):**
- External — `Stop re-pasting your context every session.`
- Internal — `You shouldn't be your AI's memory.`
- Philosophical — `Your knowledge belongs to you. Even when an AI uses it.`

## Prompt 3 — Empathy-Authority Positioner

**Empathy statement (mirrors the internal problem, not generic):**
> You're already running the same Slack threads, doc links, and
> code snippets through three different AI tools every week. You'd
> love to stop. We get it — being your AI's memory is exhausting,
> and it's not what you signed up for.

**Three authority proof points to display:**
1. **Measured benchmark numbers** — `0.9268 R@5` on LongMemEval-S
   50-distractor split (within 0.1pp of ByteRover, 0.6pp behind
   agentmemory), `0.7202 NDCG@10` on BEIR SciFact (5,183 docs ×
   300 queries) — retrieval-only, no LLM judge, on the actual
   public benchmark.
2. **CPU-only, ARM-capable** — runs on a $7/month Hetzner ARM box
   with 4 GB RAM, on your laptop, on a Raspberry Pi. No GPU
   required. No NVIDIA dependency.
3. **Open weights, open source** — every component (Xenova ONNX
   embedders, `sqlite-vec`, the Rust `embed-server` fastembed
   binary) is reproducible from public artifacts. No managed-
   service lock-in.

**Guide introduction (2 sentences, leads with empathy, closes with authority — About-page copy):**
> We were the same engineers re-explaining the same context to
> Claude every session — we built wellinformed because the
> alternative was paying $200/mo for someone else to own our
> memory. Now it runs on the laptop you already have, federates
> with your other devices and trusted peers, and matches every
> retrieval benchmark mem0 and ByteRover publish — for $0/mo on
> commodity hardware.

## Prompt 5 — Action Trigger

**Direct CTA (3-5 words, action verb first):**
> **Install in 30 seconds**

**Transitional CTA (free resource that solves a small version of the external problem):**
> **Download the LongMemEval bench results** — see exactly how
> wellinformed compares to mem0, agentmemory, and ByteRover on the
> public benchmark, with the full per-question-type breakdown.

**Button placement strategy:**
- Direct CTA appears 3× on the homepage:
  1. Top-right (sticky header, all pages)
  2. Center-fold (right after the hero subheadline)
  3. End of every doc page
- Transitional CTA appears 3× more gently:
  1. Below the hero (alternative to the direct CTA for not-yet-ready buyers)
  2. Sidebar on docs pages
  3. Footer of every page
- Direct CTA appears BEFORE transitional CTA visually — confidence
  leads, generosity follows.

## Prompt 6 — Stakes Amplifier

**What specific negative outcome happens if they do nothing?**
> They continue paying for AI subscriptions whose "memory"
> features are either nonexistent, gated behind enterprise tiers,
> or scheduled to be deprecated. They continue being the human
> glue between every conversation. The cost compounds quietly.

**What does life look like in 6 months without solving this?**
> The same provider they're paying today will have changed
> something — pricing, privacy terms, or deprecation of the
> feature they depend on. The context they've built through
> dozens of conversations doesn't come with them. It stays with
> the provider. They start each new session re-typing the same
> background they typed last week.

**What's the cost of inaction in dollars, time, or status?**
- ~$20-200/mo in unnecessary AI subscriptions and re-research API
  tokens
- ~30 min/day re-establishing context = ~15 hours/month of pure
  cognitive overhead
- Zero leverage when the provider changes terms — knowledge curated
  through your work doesn't transfer

**Stakes paragraph (for the sales page):**
> Every week you wait, you're paying twice — once for the AI
> subscription, once with the time you spend being its memory.
> Six months from now, the same provider you're paying today will
> have changed something — pricing, privacy, or the feature you
> depend on. And when they do, the context you've built through
> dozens of conversations doesn't come with you. It stays with
> them. You don't have to keep building on rented ground.

> spent Thursday debugging, the architectural decision you made
> three weeks ago — it remembers all of it, and pulls the right
> piece in the moment you need it.
>
> You're not your AI's memory anymore.
>
> When you switch to Cursor or your terminal, the same memory
> follows. When the provider changes their terms, your knowledge
> doesn't go with them. When you're offline on a flight,
> everything still works.
>
> You stopped paying twice — once for the subscription, once with
> your time. You took back the layer of your work that mattered
> most.

## Notes for the rewrite

- **The brand name `wellinformed` is missing from the headline on purpose.** StoryBrand explicitly forbids putting the brand name in the headline — the headline is the customer's desire, stated plainly. The brand earns the mention later.
- **The authority proof points are real numbers, not "trusted by X teams" puffery.** That matters — every other agent-memory company is selling the same vision; we have measured benchmark numbers to back it.
- **The stakes paragraph names the actual cost of doing nothing** — re-paying subscriptions, re-establishing context every week, losing leverage when the provider changes terms. This is honest consequence, not fear-mongering.
- **The success section uses present tense and "you"** — the reader sees themselves in it. This is the StoryBrand rule that turns features into identity shift.
