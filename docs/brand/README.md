# Folklore — Brand

The one-liner, the look, the voice. Everything downstream (site, art, merch, social) inherits from here.

## Positioning

**What it is:** a local-first memory + research layer for AI agents that answers before the web and federates peer-to-peer.

**One-liner:** *Your agent never researches the same thing twice.*

**The bet (say it in this order):** it works **alone** on day one; it **compounds** when peers join. Product first, protocol second. We are not selling a standard and hoping for adopters — we ship a working, benchmarked tool, and the spec documents what it already does.

**Why "Folklore":** knowledge that survives by being passed on — story to story, peer to peer — never relearned from scratch. The lore is the graph; the folk are the peers.

## Logo

The mark is a **hearth and its tales**: a warm central node — the fire everyone gathers around — with a loose constellation of story-nodes radiating out and linked to each other. The bright links run hearth-to-tale (knowledge leaving the source); the faint links run tale-to-tale (the story being passed on through the network). It reads as both a campfire and a peer graph, which is the whole idea.

- **File:** [`assets/folklore-logo.svg`](../../assets/folklore-logo.svg)
- **Construction:** rounded-square dark field (`#0d1117`), white nodes + links, one warm accent on the hearth.
- **Why it works small:** at favicon size it collapses to "a warm dot in a web of dots" — legible, ownable, not another generic spark or brain.

### Color

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#0d1117` | backgrounds, the logo field |
| Bone | `#ffffff` | nodes, links, primary text on ink |
| Hearth | `#ff9f43` | the one accent — fire, CTAs, highlights. Use sparingly. |

The discipline: **monochrome plus one warm accent.** The warmth is the brand — folklore is human, gathered, told. Resist the cold blue/purple "AI protocol" default; that's the look we're differentiating against.

### Usage

- **Do:** keep clear space around the mark equal to one node's diameter; use the ink field as the org/social avatar.
- **Don't:** recolor the hearth to blue, add gradients to the links, or stretch the mark.

## Voice

Plainspoken, confident, a little folk-warm. We explain by metaphor (hearth, tales, the commons, stone soup) and back every claim with a falsifiable number. We never oversell — the proof section does the bragging.

- **Say:** "answered from your own graph in milliseconds", "nobody pays for it twice", "signed by a real, named human".
- **Avoid:** "revolutionary", "paradigm", cold protocol-speak, claims without a benchmark behind them.
