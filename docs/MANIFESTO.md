# The Folklore Manifesto

*Before libraries, before print, before anyone owned a copy of anything, knowledge survived one way: it was retold. Around fires, on roads, mouth to ear — every telling made the next teller richer, and no one paid twice for a story the village already knew. We are building that, for machine reasoning.*

---

## 1. Reasoning is labor.

Every conclusion an agent reaches was paid for — in tokens, in electricity, in time. That work has value after the session ends. An answer that dies with its context window is labor thrown into the sea. We keep what we pay for.

## 2. Every re-derivation is a tax.

The same paper is read a million times. The same bug is chased down the same dead-ends, on the same night, by strangers who will never know they shared it. Each repetition is billed as if it were new — a tax paid to the same few companies, forever, for reasoning the world already owns. Folklore is tax refusal by memory.

## 3. Knowledge wants to travel mouth to ear.

Centralized platforms taught us that knowledge must live in one place, behind one gate, ranked by one algorithm. That is not how knowledge ever worked. It moves peer to peer, teller to listener, each carrying their own version, each accountable for what they pass on. The oldest protocol is still the best one.

## 4. The network is smarter than the node.

No single agent, however large its model, has walked all the dead-ends your peers have walked this week. Freshness beats scale: a thousand small graphs, each holding what its owner actually verified yesterday, out-know any frozen snapshot of the internet. Together, peers form a mind no datacenter can train.

## 5. Shared memory shrinks the model.

The industry's answer to every gap is a bigger model — more parameters, more inference, more energy, more dependence. There is another axis: remember more, infer less. An agent that starts from the network's resolved reasoning needs fewer tokens to reach further. Shared memory is the road to smaller, local, decentralized models — intelligence at the edge, not rented from the center.

## 6. Less inference is a feature.

Every avoided re-derivation is compute that was never burned, a datacenter-second that was never spent, a bill that was never sent. Efficiency here is not a discount — it is independence. The less inference you must buy, the less permission you must ask.

## 7. No big brother in the loop.

When one company hosts the memory, ranks the answers, and meters the reasoning, it does not merely serve the network — it *is* the network, and you are a tenant. Folklore has no landlord. Your graph lives on your disk. Your peers are chosen by you. Nothing about what you know, ask, or share transits an authority. Dependence on the center is a decision, not a default — and we decide no.

## 8. Provenance over authority.

The centralized web launders knowledge: scraped, blended, served back with the names washed off. In folklore every record is signed by a cryptographic identity, tied to a named hand, timestamped, traceable to its sources. Trust is not granted by a platform — it is earned per teller, verifiable per claim. Attribution is not a courtesy. It is the security model.

## 9. Local-first is sovereignty.

It works alone, on your machine, on day one — no account, no cloud, no quorum. The network multiplies the value; it never holds it hostage. If every peer but you vanished tonight, you would lose nothing you made. That is the test of ownership, and most of the tools you rent today fail it.

## 10. The commons compounds.

A torrent gets healthier with every seeder. So does this: every question resolved anywhere becomes a question nobody pays for again — you on Thursday, a stranger in Osaka next year. Selfishness and generosity collapse into the same act, because serving the network and serving yourself are the same motion. The fire gets warmer with every hand that feeds it.

---

**We keep what we pay for. We sign what we share. We answer from the network before the web, and from the web only once.**

**Never research twice — anywhere on the network.**
