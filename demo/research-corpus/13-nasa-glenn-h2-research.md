# NASA Glenn Research Center — Plum Brook Station H2 program

NASA Glenn's Plum Brook Station has been the U.S. government's primary H2 propellant
test facility since 1968. The site has a 100 m³ vacuum-jacketed LH2 storage tank, a
50 m³ working tank, a transfer line that sees 200+ fill/drain cycles per year, and an
extensive instrumentation rig that's been continuously upgraded since 2017.

**Recent publication record (last 3 years):**

- *NASA TM-2023-0149* — "Standoff Raman LIDAR for H2 leak detection: Plum Brook
  open-air rig". 50 ppm sensitivity at 30 m. Reference paper for the Stanford
  Cryo Lab's denoiser work. See `02-laser-raman-leak-detection.md`.

- *NASA TM-2022-0387* — "Inline QMS for vent-line monitoring: 18-month operational
  review". Documented the heated-capillary fix for moisture condensation. See
  `04-mass-spec-effluent.md`.

- *NASA TM-2023-0244* — "Acoustic emission detection sensitivity at LH2 temperatures:
  systematic survey at Plum Brook Station". 8–12 dB AE drop at cryogenic conditions.
  See `06-acoustic-emission-h2.md`.

- *NASA TM-2022-0567* — "Boil-off rate measurement: sources of error and a Kalman-
  filter approach". Catalogued ±1 %/day stratification error. See
  `08-boil-off-rate-monitoring.md`.

**Open-data releases:** NASA Glenn has released ~4 GB of calibration runs from the
2021–2024 Raman LIDAR campaigns, plus ~12 GB of synced sensor traces from the 2023
Plum Brook AE survey. Both are at `15-public-h2-sensor-datasets.md`. Use is governed
by the NASA Open Data Agreement — derivative work must include a citation back to the
relevant TM number.

**Personnel:** the H2 sensor program is led by Dr. K. Liu (since 2019), with
~6 PhD-level engineers and a rotating 2–4 postdoc cohort. Day-to-day site operations
are run by the Plum Brook engineering staff (~30 FTE) which are NASA contractors
rather than research staff.

**Collaboration model:** Plum Brook Station accepts external research proposals via
NASA's standard mechanism. The Stanford Cryogenic Systems Lab and ETH Zurich aerospace
LH2 groups have both run on-site campaigns there. Industry partners go through the
Glenn technology-transfer office.
