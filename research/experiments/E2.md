# E2 — Does the energy/OOD admission gate protect against on-distribution-but-STALE hits?

## Objective & hypothesis

The energy gate (`src/domain/energy-gate.ts`) admits a cache hit when
`−E(q) = −T·logsumexp(simᵢ/T) ≥ τ` **and** `Δ = sim₍₁₎−sim₍₂₎ ≥ sepMin` **and**
`hits ≥ minHits` (defaults `T=0.1, τ=−0.016, sepMin=0.01, minHits=2`). Those τ/β
were fitted on the deny-real OOD split — in-corpus vs topically-absent — and score
AUC 0.78 there (`energy-gate.ts:33-40`). E2 attacks the orthogonal axis the gate
was never trained on: a hit that is on-distribution (high `simᵢ`, high-confidence
admit) but factually stale (its fact has since been revised).

**Falsifiable hypothesis (H1).** Among hits the energy gate *admits*, downstream
answer error rises monotonically as ground-truth staleness rises, but admission
confidence `−E(q)` does **not** track that error: the Spearman correlation between
`−E(q)` and downstream error among admitted-stale hits is ≈ 0, i.e. the
**error-by-confidence curve is flat**. Equivalently, `−E(q)` separates fresh-correct
from stale-wrong at **AUC ≤ 0.6** (vs the 0.78 it earns on OOD). If so, the gate
gives no staleness protection and the freshness multiplier `φ`
(`peer-telemetry.ts:363`) is the only — and currently uncalibrated — line of defense.

## What this reuses vs what to build

**Reused as-is.**
- `src/domain/energy-gate.ts` — `energyGate(sims, params)` and `freeEnergy`. Called
  directly on `simᵢ = 1 − vec_distance_i` (its documented contract, `energy-gate.ts:110-113`).
- `tests/bench-longmemeval-real.test.ts` + `tests/bench-locomo-real.test.ts` — the
  in-process index→search path (`indexNode`, `searchGlobal`, `rerankMatches`,
  scratch graph + `openSqliteVectorIndex` per question). These already parse the
  real datasets, including `question_type`, `question_date`, `haystack_dates`,
  `answer_session_ids` (LongMemEval) and `category` (LoCoMo). Gated by
  `FOLKLORE_BENCH_PUBLIC_REAL=1`, `LONGMEMEVAL_DIR`/`LOCOMO_DIR`.
- `src/domain/eval-metrics.ts` — `recallAtK`, `recallAnyAtK`, `reciprocalRank`.
- `src/domain/peer-telemetry.ts` — `freshnessGate` / `FRESH_FLOOR=0.3` /
  `DEFAULT_STALE_AFTER_DAYS=7` (the `φ` we recalibrate).
- The Mann-Whitney `auc()` and Youden τ-sweep from `bench/bench-energy-gate.mjs`
  (copied, not re-derived).

**To build (new).**
- `bench/bench-energy-stale.mjs` — a sibling of `bench-energy-gate.mjs`. Per query
  it retrieves over a haystack containing *both* the old and the revised fact, calls
  `energyGate()` on the survivor sims, and records `{negEnergy, separation, admit}`,
  the age of the top admitted hit, and a correctness label (current vs superseded
  fact). It is in-process (reusing the LongMemEval test's index path), **not** the
  CLI path — `ask --json`'s energy field (`ask.ts:215`) can read a verdict but
  cannot inject controlled staleness, so we adapt to the in-process harness. *This
  is the one deviation from `bench-energy-gate.mjs`, which runs `dist/cli/index.js
  ask`.*
- `eval/fixtures/stale-real/` — via a new `generate-stale-fixtures.mjs` (mirrors
  `deny-real/generate-fixtures.mjs`: manifest + jsonl + provenance).
- `freshnessCalibrate()` — a fitter in the new bench gridding
  `FRESH_FLOOR ∈ {0.1..0.5}` × `half_life ∈ {1,3,7,14,30}d` against measured
  `age_days → P(stale-wrong)`, reporting ECE; it does **not** edit
  `peer-telemetry.ts` until a fit lands.

## Dataset / fixtures

Primary source: **LongMemEval-S `knowledge-update` category** — questions whose
answer changed across sessions (the `question_date` post-dates an earlier,
now-superseded session). This is the only public, in-repo-wired set with
*time-stamped fact revisions*; `REAL-MEMORY-BENCHMARKS.md` confirms our adapter
already scores it (R@5 1.000 retrieval — so retrieval is *not* the confound; the
fact is found, the question is whether the gate trusts the stale copy of it).

Construction (`generate-stale-fixtures.mjs`): per knowledge-update question, emit a
row `{query, gold_session_ids, haystack_session_ids, haystack_dates,
stale_session_id, fresh_session_id, answer}`. Staleness is **structural/measured,
not assumed**: a hit is `stale` iff its `session_date <` the fact-revision date in
`haystack_dates`. Augment with **LoCoMo** `category ∈ {1,2,3}` factual items as a
second stratum. Target: LongMemEval-S knowledge-update ≈ 78 questions, LoCoMo
factual ≈ 40 — pooled **n ≈ 118**; the *admitted-AND-stale* sub-base is the scarce
cell (§Statistics).

## Variables

**Independent (manipulated).** (a) Ground-truth staleness of the top admitted hit
(`fresh` vs `stale`, from `haystack_dates`); (b) the freshness policy — `OFF`
(energy gate alone), `φ-default` (`FRESH_FLOOR=0.3`, half-life 7d), and `φ-fitted`.

**Dependent (code-level).** `downstream_error ∈ {0,1}` = top admitted hit carries the
superseded fact (computed by the new bench from `stale_session_id` membership of the
top hit). Aggregated:
- `err_by_conf[bin]` — error rate bucketed by `−E(q)` deciles (new code in
  `bench-energy-stale.mjs`).
- `AUC_stale = auc(negE[fresh-correct], negE[stale-wrong])` — reuses
  `bench-energy-gate.mjs:79-84`.
- `ECE_φ` — expected calibration error of `φ·−E(q)` against `downstream_error`,
  10-bin, computed in `freshnessCalibrate()`.

## Baseline

Two bracketing oracles (the RQ note's bracket): **reject-all-stale** (upper bound:
every stale hit dropped, perfect age oracle) and **admit-all** (lower bound: no gate).
The honest *reference arm* is **energy-gate-only with no φ** (`FOLKLORE_ENERGY_GATE=1`,
freshness disabled): it isolates exactly what admission scoring buys before the
freshness multiplier is credited.

## Procedure

1. `npm run build` (the in-process harness imports `dist/domain/energy-gate.js`).
2. Download datasets per `REAL-MEMORY-BENCHMARKS.md` (LongMemEval-S to `$DIR`,
   `locomo10.json`).
3. `node eval/fixtures/stale-real/generate-stale-fixtures.mjs` → writes
   `in-stale.jsonl` + `manifest.json` (with `graph_nodes`, `seed`, per-stratum counts,
   provenance — same shape as `deny-real/manifest.json`).
4. Reference arm: `FOLKLORE_BENCH_PUBLIC_REAL=1 LONGMEMEVAL_DIR=$DIR LOCOMO_DIR=$DIR \
   node bench/bench-energy-stale.mjs --k 5 --freshness off`.
5. Treatment arms: rerun with `--freshness default` and `--freshness fit`.
6. Per arm the bench prints: admit-rate fresh vs stale, `err_by_conf` table,
   `AUC_stale` (vs the 0.78 OOD anchor), Youden τ on the fresh-vs-stale axis, and
   `freshnessCalibrate()`'s best `(FRESH_FLOOR, half_life)` + `ECE_φ`; writes
   `eval/out/energy-stale-summary.json` (mirrors `deny-real-summary.json`).
7. Bootstrap CIs (§Statistics) over the saved per-query rows.

## Statistics & sample size

Test: H1 dies if `AUC_stale ≥ 0.7` OR Spearman(`−E`, error) is significantly
negative. Use a **1000-sample bootstrap** over per-query rows for the AUC CI (the
deny-real harness already warns one flipped query moves a rate ~3 pts at n≈30, so we
report CIs, not point rates). Power note: the binding cell is *admitted-AND-stale*.
With pooled n≈118 and an expected admit-rate ~0.57 and stale-fraction ~0.4, the
stale-admitted base is ≈ **27 queries** — underpowered to resolve AUC differences
< ~0.15 at 80% power. Mitigation: (i) pool LongMemEval + LoCoMo; (ii) lower
`minHits` to 1 only for a sensitivity arm; (iii) pre-register the bench and report
the CI, treating a *flat curve with wide CI* as "no evidence of protection," not
proof of its absence — matching the RQ2 framing of a clean null.

## Honest-metrics discipline

Every number is **measured** (retrieval + energy verdict over real MiniLM-384
embeddings) — there is **no simulator arm**. Staleness labels are structural (date
comparison), never inferred from the gate. The gate is compared to its own OOD score
(0.78) on the *same* Mann-Whitney AUC, so the OOD-vs-stale contrast is matched. The
reject-all-stale oracle is an upper bound, never reported as achievable. φ is
"calibrated" only once `ECE_φ` beats the `φ-default` arm on held-out queries; the
reference arm is the *shipped* energy gate, not a strawman static threshold.

## The falsifier

H1 is **refuted** if, among admitted hits, `−E(q)` separates fresh-correct from
stale-wrong at **AUC_stale ≥ 0.70** with the bootstrap 95% CI lower bound > 0.60 —
i.e. the error-by-confidence curve has a real downward slope and the gate *does*
protect against staleness. Conversely H1 is **confirmed** (the dangerous outcome) if
`AUC_stale ≤ 0.60` and high-confidence admitted-stale hits carry **≥** the error of
rejected hits — the flat curve — proving the OOD gate transfers zero protection to
staleness and that an uncalibrated `φ` stands alone.

## Threats to validity

1. **Tiny stale-admitted base (~27).** The dominant threat; addressed by pooling
   two datasets and reporting bootstrap CIs rather than point AUCs.
2. **Retrieval/staleness confound.** If retrieval simply fails to surface the fresh
   fact, "error" measures recall, not gate trust. Controlled by requiring both
   stale+fresh sessions in the haystack and gating analysis on questions where
   `recall_any@5 = 1` (the fresh fact *was* retrieved) — feasible since
   knowledge-update R@5 = 1.000.
3. **Date-derived label noise.** `haystack_dates` granularity and the in-process
   `fetched_at` default (`bench-longmemeval-real.test.ts:247`) can misorder
   near-simultaneous revisions; mitigated by dropping questions whose stale/fresh
   gap < 1 day and recording the dropped count in the manifest.
