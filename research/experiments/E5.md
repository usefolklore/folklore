# E5 — Compounding at scale: the 100-peer 30-day pilot (+ honest sim baseline)

## Objective & hypothesis

RQ5 claims compounding is a **monotone-submodular coverage** process: marginal
value of the *n*-th shared inference decays with diminishing returns (`(1−1/e)`
greedy bound), reliable because retrieval *precision*, not network size, bounds
it. The simulators we already ship (`bench/bench-memtool-federation.py` and
`-scifact.py`) report cache hit-rate that is **monotone-up by construction**
(`silo` flat in N, `federated` rising) and therefore *cannot* exhibit capacity
collapse, churn loss, or a growing false-admit tail. Only a live pilot measures
those. **Falsifiable hypothesis:** over 30 days, per-user *net task-quality lift*
plotted against *cumulative shared-node count* is **concave/saturating** (fits a
`a·(1−e^{−bx})` coverage curve better than a line, ΔAIC ≥ 2), **while** the
real-population false-admit rate stays **below the measured real-geometry bound
(SciFact ≤15%, energy-gate AUC 0.78 — `src/domain/energy-gate.ts:36`)**, not the
≤1% simulator bound.

## What this reuses vs what to build

**Reused as-is.**
- `bench/bench-compounding-real.mjs` — the only harness that runs the *real*
  energy gate (`energyGate`/`freeEnergy` from `dist/domain/energy-gate.js`) over
  real BEIR corpus+qrels with `--churn` and cooperative CRDT-style replication.
  Its `runArm()` emits exactly E5's dependent variables: `fallback_start/_end`
  (web_fallback curve), `correct_resolve_rate`, `false_admit_rate`, `web_paid`,
  `compute_saved_tok`. **This is the offline twin / pre-registration arm.** Flags:
  `--dataset --peers --steps --cap --churn --json`.
- `bench/bench-memtool-federation.py` + `-scifact.py` — the **honest SIMULATOR
  baseline** (self-labelled `kind: "SIMULATOR"`), unchanged, drawing the
  by-construction monotone curve the pilot is measured *against*.
- `src/domain/energy-gate.ts` — live admission decision; defaults `τ=−0.016,
  sepMin=0.01, T=0.1` (`ENERGY_GATE_DEFAULTS`).
- Fixtures `eval/fixtures/scifact-mini/` (40 corpus / 18 queries / 19 qrels) for
  the calibration smoke; full SciFact under `~/.folklore/bench/scifact/scifact/`.

**To build (new).**
1. **`bench/pilot-telemetry.mjs`** (new) — a node-side hook that, per resolved
   query, appends one JSONL row `{peer_id, t, qid_or_hash, admit, top_sim,
   neg_energy, separation, age_days, cum_shared_nodes, web_fallback, graded}` to
   `~/.folklore/pilot/<peer>.jsonl`. Slots in beside the existing PostToolUse
   auto-save hook; reads the same `energyGate` verdict the gate already computed.
2. **`bench/pilot-lift-scorer.mjs`** (new) — offline aggregator. For each user it
   bins rows by `cum_shared_nodes`, computes `Δquality = quality(reuse) −
   quality(single-peer baseline)` per bin, and fits both a line and
   `a·(1−e^{−bx})` (least squares), reporting ΔAIC and the per-bin false-admit
   tail. This is the curve the falsifier reads. Reuses
   `bench-compounding-real.mjs`'s `correct/falseAdmit` accounting logic.
3. **An answer-quality grader** (not retrieval `recall@1`): LLM-graded
   correctness against the gold qrel answer, identical scorer to the one E1
   builds, so E1/E5 quality numbers are comparable. Stale items get
   time-stamped labels from the LongMemEval/LoCoMo knowledge-update adapters
   (per E2).

## Dataset / fixtures

Two coupled streams. **(a) Offline twin:** full SciFact (and NFCorpus for
heterogeneity) via the BEIR wiring `bench-compounding-real.mjs` loads
(`corpus.jsonl`, `queries.jsonl`, `qrels/{test,dev}.tsv`), demand drawn Zipfian
(`drawQ`, exponent 0.9) to force reuse. **(b) Live pilot:** 100 opt-in peers
running real coding/research sessions for 30 days; their organic
`WebSearch`/`WebFetch` queries are the demand. Gold labels for the *graded*
subset come from qrels (where a query maps to a BEIR item) and LongMemEval/LoCoMo
time-stamped fact revisions (staleness). Per-peer cumulative shared-node count is
read from the CRDT (`private:false` nodes only).

## Variables

- **Independent:** cumulative shared-node count per user over 30 days (live);
  mirrored offline by `--steps` (demand volume) at `--peers {1,16,100}` and
  `--churn {0,0.2,0.5}`.
- **Dependent (code-level):**
  - *web_fallback fraction* = `webPaid/STEPS` per 20-bucket trajectory
    (`runArm` `series[]`, exposed as `fallback_start/_end`).
  - *false_admit_rate* = `falseAdmit/hits` (`runArm:216`) — admit but top cached
    doc NOT qrel-relevant.
  - *net task-quality lift* = `Δquality` from the new grader, **not**
    `correct_resolve_rate` (which is retrieval-grounded). New code:
    `pilot-lift-scorer.mjs` computes `lift(x) = quality_coop(x) −
    quality_single(x)` per cum-shared bin x.

## Baseline

The **honest reference is a matched single-peer arm**: the same user's identical
query stream with federation disabled (`--peers 1`, or live `FOLKLORE_PREFETCH_PEERS=0`).
This is exactly the `isolated` arm of `runArm(cooperative=false,…)`. The
SIMULATOR federation curve (`bench-memtool-federation*.py`) is reported *only* as
the by-construction monotone upper-envelope to contrast against the measured
curve — never as the pilot's success criterion.

## Procedure

1. Calibrate offline: `node bench/bench-compounding-real.mjs --dataset scifact
   --json` → record `calibration.{tau,sepMin,tpr,fpr}` and **lock** them as the
   pilot's gate params (no live re-tuning, to keep the curve honest).
2. Pre-register the twin curve: `for P in 1 16 100; do for C in 0 0.2 0.5; do
   node bench/bench-compounding-real.mjs --peers $P --churn $C --steps 6000
   --json; done; done` — the *expected* shapes before any live data.
3. Draw the SIMULATOR envelope: `python3
   bench/bench-memtool-federation-scifact.py --json` and `python3
   bench/bench-memtool-federation.py --json`.
4. Deploy 100 peers with `bench/pilot-telemetry.mjs` enabled; run 30 days on the
   shipped Y.js CRDT sync, real churn and demand.
5. Nightly: `node bench/pilot-lift-scorer.mjs --in ~/.folklore/pilot --grade` →
   per-user `lift(cum_shared)` curve + false-admit tail.
6. Day-30: fit line vs `a·(1−e^{−bx})` per user; aggregate ΔAIC + false-admit
   trajectory; compare measured web_fallback curve to the step-2 twin.

## Statistics & sample size

n = 100 users × ~30 daily curve points. Per-user model selection by **ΔAIC**
(saturating vs linear); population test that median ΔAIC > 2 via a one-sided
**Wilcoxon signed-rank** (non-normal, paired per user). False-admit drift tested
as a **Mann-Kendall trend** over the 30-day daily rate. **Feasibility caveat
(RQ5):** the clean-correct base is small — SciFact yields only 18 mini / ~300
full graded pairs and the gate's Youden point admits ~57% (`energy-gate.ts:36`),
so the *graded* subset per user may be tens, not thousands. Mitigation: pool the
graded subset across users for the population false-admit estimate (report a
Wilson 95% CI), keep the per-user lift curve on the larger *retrieval-grounded*
proxy, and power for detecting a ≥0.05 correct-resolve gap (the
`bench-compounding-real.mjs:241` `compounds` threshold) rather than a precise
slope.

## Honest-metrics discipline

Every figure tagged **SIMULATOR** (the federation `.py` curves, monotone by
construction) vs **MEASURED** (pilot + offline-twin energy-gate). Comparisons are
**matched**: cooperative vs single-peer on the *same* stream, *same* locked
`τ/sepMin`. Quality is graded against **gold qrels / time-stamped revisions,
never the cache gate**. No weak-baseline inflation: the baseline is a
*vCache-quality* single node, not "no cache". The simulator's ~90%@64-peer hit
rate is never promoted to a deployment claim.

## The falsifier

Refute the hypothesis if **either**: (a) the per-user lift curve is
**linear/non-saturating** — population-median ΔAIC(saturating − linear) **< 2**,
or the fitted `b` indistinguishable from 0 — which kills the submodular-coverage
transfer from S-RAG; **or** (b) lift goes **flat-or-negative while precision
degrades** — daily false-admit rate shows a significant rising Mann-Kendall trend
**drifting toward/past the SciFact ≤15% bound** as cum-shared-nodes grows, i.e.
recall up but `Δquality ≤ 0`. Either refutes reliable compounding.

## Threats to validity

1. **Confound: demand correlation, not coverage.** If peers query near-identical
   things, lift saturates trivially. Mitigate with the NFCorpus heterogeneity arm
   and `--churn 0.5` stress; report per-user demand entropy alongside lift.
2. **Small graded base → wide false-admit CIs** (the RQ5 weak link). The ≤15%
   bound may sit inside the CI; pool across users and report Wilson intervals,
   not point estimates.
3. **Selection / Hawthorne.** 100 opt-in power-users aren't the general
   population, and instrumentation may shift behavior; the offline twin (step 2)
   anchors the *same gate* under controlled demand, so live drift is attributable
   to population, not harness.
