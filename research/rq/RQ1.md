## RQ1 — Reuse beats fresh when you match on the question, verify the payload, and add it as context — and the similarity gate is the whole ballgame

**Answer.** Reusing a prior answer outperforms answering fresh in a narrow, characterizable regime: (1) the match is made on the *question* side, not the document side; (2) the reused payload is actually correct; (3) it enters as *additive context* the model can still reason over, not a verbatim replacement it must blindly trust; and (4) the similarity gate is tuned to the encoder/task distribution rather than a portable constant. Outside that regime — diverse/unstructured queries, oversized context, or a static threshold set too loose — reuse either fails to fire or, worse, injects a confidently-wrong near-match.

**What the literature shows.** Two families. *Reuse-as-context*: CAG (2412.15605) preloads the whole knowledge set into the KV cache and skips retrieval, beating sparse/dense RAG on **BERTScore** at small/medium scale (HotPotQA 0.795 vs 0.768/0.758) and running 10.9×–40.7× faster — but the margin inverts once knowledge outgrows the window (HotPotQA-Large 0.7407 vs Sparse-RAG 0.7463). GSA (2503.04104) aggregates an LLM's *own* sampled responses into a new answer, gaining +2.5–5.3 points (GSM8K +3.58, MATH +3.18, GPQA +2.90, SVAMP +5.33) and ~+8 win-rate on open-ended Alpaca Eval — but only as *synthesis*; using the LLM to *judge/select* among responses (self-refine) sometimes underperforms greedy (GPQA 41.07→40.03), and gains peak at N=5 then decay. *Reuse-as-replacement* (semantic caching): GPT Semantic Cache (2411.05276) gates on cosine ≥ 0.8, hitting 61.6–68.8% of queries at 92.5–97.3% positive-hit accuracy, and explicitly reports that *below* 0.8 you buy more hits at the cost of irrelevant matches. MeanCache (2403.02694) shows the optimal gate is model-dependent (0.83 for MPNet, 0.78 for Albert) and that GPTCache's 0.7 is too loose; crucially, ignoring conversational context yields 54 false hits vs 3 once context chains are encoded (precision 0.66→0.98). vCache (2502.03771) makes the threshold the object of study: a single static cutoff gives no correctness guarantee, so it learns a *per-prompt* threshold via logistic regression on (similarity, correctness) pairs and delivers a user-set bound Pr(correct) ≥ 1−δ (δ = 1/2/3%), reporting up to 12.5× higher hit rate and 26× lower error than static baselines. The papers agree on the core hazard — false hits from sub-threshold-but-different-need matches — and disagree only on the fix: a better constant (MeanCache) vs. a learned per-prompt bound (vCache).

**How it maps to Folklore.** Folklore's inference-tree sharing is a *reuse-as-replacement* system whose central bet — match on the answered-question pool (q2q), inherit the verified doc — is exactly what the caching literature says makes reuse reliable. MeanCache's query-side matching and vCache's framing **CONFIRM** that the question-to-question channel is the safe one; Folklore's measured **retrieval recall@1** jumps (NFCorpus 47.1→97.7%, SciFact 46.1→98.3%, FiQA 34.9→94.7%, hurt=0) are consistent with the claim that q↔q similarity (paraphrase floor ~0.71–0.84) dominates q↔doc. But the literature also **CHALLENGES** one knob: Folklore's recalibrated gate of 0.6 sits *below* every reliable cache threshold in these papers (0.78–0.83, 0.8). Cosine cutoffs aren't portable across encoders, so this is not a literal contradiction — but the *direction* is the one GPT Semantic Cache flagged as accuracy-eroding, and 0.6 lands inside Folklore's own [0.6, 0.71) no-man's-land between the gate and its paraphrase floor. vCache **EXTENDS** Folklore cleanly: replace the single 0.6 with a per-question learned threshold and Folklore's empirical "hurt=0" becomes a *guaranteed* error ≤ δ. CAG/GSA add a guardrail — because Folklore inherits one verified doc it stays in the safe small-context regime, but multi-tree inheritance would hit CAG's narrowing gap and GSA's N=5 ceiling.

**Open questions / the falsifier.** The unresolved gap: Folklore's gains and "hurt=0" are measured as *retrieval recall@1 on BEIR*, but RQ1 asks about *answer quality*. Every risk the literature names — false hits at a loose gate, the unverified correctness of the reused answer — lives downstream of retrieval, so recall can read 97% while end-to-end answer quality silently regresses. The falsifier: assemble query pairs with cosine in [0.6, 0.71) where the information *need* genuinely differs, run them end-to-end, and score answer correctness vs. fresh. If reuse in that band degrades answers above Folklore's tolerance — i.e., hurt > 0 once you score answers instead of retrieval — the 0.6 gate is falsified and must rise toward 0.78–0.83 or go per-prompt (vCache). The "verified-tree" premise is the second falsifier: no recall number here or in the cache papers proves the reused answer was *right* — only that a near-match was found.
